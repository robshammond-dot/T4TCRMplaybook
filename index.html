2Enquiry received & agent introduction

Two emails covering the gap between an enquiry landing and a named agent taking it over. This is the very first thing a client hears from us, so it carries the most weight for setting expectations honestly.

Two-step today because assignment is manual and can take a while. Once round robin is live and assigning straight from form submission (see the Auto assignment section under Team knowledge & round robin), the wait between these two emails shrinks close to zero, at which point the first email can be retired and "Meet your agent" becomes the sole first-touch send.
Email 1 · Enquiry received
"Your enquiry's been shared with our agents."
Alt: "We've got your {{destination}} enquiry."
Sent immediately on form submission, before anyone is assigned
Job: confirm the enquiry landed and set a realistic timeline. States office hours plainly (so a Friday evening enquiry doesn't sit wondering why nobody's replied by Saturday morning) and gives a genuine expectation for when an agent will be in touch, not a vague "shortly."
Email 2 · Meet your agent
"Meet {{agent_name}}, your travel expert."
Alt: "Say hello to {{agent_name}}."
Sent the moment the deal is assigned to an agent
Job: put a real name and face to the enquiry and set the agent up to succeed. Photo, short personal line, and a clear "here's what happens next" so the client's first real interaction feels like a handoff, not a cold start.

3Follow-up sequences

Two separate sequences, triggered at different points in the journey. Consultation follow-up fires when someone has enquired but no quote exists yet. Quote follow-up fires once a formal quote has gone out. Each sequence has its own colour family so agents can tell at a glance which stage a client is in.

Consultation follow-up2 sends · no quote issued yet
Consultation 1 · Same day
"Still thinking it over? We've got you."
Alt: "Your {{destination}} plans, picked up right where you left them."
Sent 2–4 hours after an unanswered enquiry / consultation
Job: reassure, not chase. Warm acknowledgement that the enquiry hasn't been forgotten, with the agent's name attached so it reads as personal, not automated.
Consultation 2 · Day 2–3
"A few things worth knowing before you book."
Alt: "Here's what other travellers to {{destination}} ask us first."
Sent if no response 48–72 hours after Consultation 1
Job: add value, not pressure. One genuinely useful piece of info (best time to travel, a popular add-on, a common question) that gives them a reason to move to a quote. Sequence closes here — no third consultation nudge.

"Any questions on the quote?"

Sent if the quote hasn't been accepted 3–4 days after Quote 1

Final send, timed to the quote's actual expiry date where one exists
Job: gentle close. States the expiry date factually if there is one — that's real information, not manufactured urgency — and makes it easy to extend or ask for a fresh quote.

5Bon Voyage

Sent shortly before departure. "Bon Voyage" is what everyone on the team already calls it, so the internal template name stays as is — these are alternative headline options for the banner itself, not a rename.

Suggested banner headlines

  • "Your bags are (almost) packed."
  • "{{destination}} is close now."
  • "Everything's set. Time to go."
  • "One more sleep till {{destination}}."

Content checklist

  • Departure date/time confirmed clearly, no scrolling required
  • Link or attachment to travel documents
  • One practical reminder (packing, weather, check-in window)
  • Agent contact details for while they're away

6Welcome Home

Sent a day or two after return. Also a name everyone already uses — alternative banner wording below for variety, not a replacement.

Suggested banner headlines

  • "How was {{destination}}?"
  • "Back home, bags full of stories."
  • "Hope the memories are still sinking in."
  • "Welcome back — we'd love to hear how it went."

Content checklist

  • Short, low-friction feedback ask (link out, not embedded form)
  • No hard sell on the next trip — a soft "when you're ready" mention is enough
  • Thank you from the named agent, not a generic sign-off

7Bulk deals & promotions

Agent-initiated sends to their own client base, built from an approved template rather than typed from scratch each time. Different rules apply here since these are marketing sends the client opted into, not lifecycle emails triggered automatically by the CRM.

Marketing consent required. These sends must only go to contacts who've opted in to marketing communication under POPIA, never the full contact list by default. The Freshsales list filter should be set to the marketing consent segment before any bulk send goes out.
Standard deal
"This week's best fares to {{destination}}."
Alt: "A deal worth a second look."
Ad hoc, agent-triggered whenever a deal comes through
Job: surface a genuine offer clearly. One destination or package per send, real price, real availability, no exaggerated claims.
Flash / limited-time deal
"Limited seats at this price."
Alt: "Ends {{deal_expiry}}, then it's gone."
Time-boxed, expiry always stated and always accurate
Job: unlike the lifecycle sequences earlier in this doc, genuine urgency is fine here since it's marketing content the client opted into. The expiry date and any seat or price limit must still be real, never manufactured.

Requirements

  • Pulled from the approved template library only, agents duplicate a template rather than build one from a blank canvas
  • Sent only to the marketing consent segment, never the full contact list
  • One deal, one destination or package per send, keep it scannable
  • Any expiry date or seat limit stated is real and matches what's actually available
  • Standard footer requirements apply in full (unsubscribe, address, agent contact)
  • Frequency capped so the same client isn't hit with multiple bulk sends in a short window, confirm the interval with Chantelle/Wendy before rollout

Approval

Small sends can go out agent to agent without a check-in. Larger sends route through a quick sign-off first, mainly to avoid two agents blasting the same list with different deals in the same week. The exact size threshold and who signs off still needs confirming, flagged for follow-up.

8Team training considerations

What needs covering before these templates go live on the team's day-to-day workflow.

Before rollout

  • Walk through which sends are automated vs which need an agent trigger, so nobody double-sends manually
  • Make the Consultation vs Quote trigger point explicit — Consultation follow-up starts on enquiry, Quote follow-up starts the moment a quote is issued and should stop the Consultation sequence if it's still running
  • Show where merge fields pull from in Freshsales, and what happens if a field is blank (e.g. no destination captured yet, or no quote expiry set)
  • Cover the "no urgency language" rule explicitly — it's a deliberate choice, not an oversight, and agents may want to add it back in
  • Clarify Bon Voyage and Welcome Home trigger points (departure date field, return date field) so timing doesn't drift
  • One live test send per agent so everyone has seen their own name/photo render correctly before it reaches a client

Ongoing

  • Short refresher when Freshmarketer changes anything platform-side (given the recent pattern of unannounced changes)
  • Quarterly check that footer details (address, contact numbers) are still current

Bulk mail sending

  • Walk through exactly where the approved deal templates live in Freshmarketer, and get every agent to duplicate one live in training rather than just watch it demoed
  • Show the difference between editing the master template (don't) and duplicating it for their own send (do), since one wrong edit can break it for everyone
  • Cover how to pull the marketing consent segment before sending, not the full contact list, and what it looks like when the filter is set correctly
  • Be explicit about the one deal, one destination rule and why real expiry dates matter more than punchy copy
  • Confirm who signs off on larger sends once that threshold is set, so agents aren't guessing
  • Show agents where to check open rates and unsubscribes after a send, and who to flag it to if numbers look off

Capturing milestone fields

  • Make it routine, not optional, to log Date of Birth off the passport copy into the new field the moment it's collected, so it doesn't get missed or left sitting in a scanned document nobody revisits
  • Train agents to recognise when a trip is an anniversary or honeymoon booking, and to log the Special Occasion Date and Type field when it comes up naturally, never as a direct question the client didn't volunteer
  • Be clear this is manual entry, not something Freshsales or Zapier fills in automatically, so it only works if it's a consistent habit across the team
  • Cover the POPIA basics on Date of Birth specifically, since it's more sensitive than most fields already being captured

Round robin groups

Round robin is the right default here. It spreads new enquiries evenly across a group, so nobody gets skipped over or over picked because they happened to be top of mind when the enquiry landed. Freshsales doesn't support workload or capacity based assignment on Deals, that's a Freshdesk feature built for support tickets, not something available on this side of the platform.

The practical middle ground: keep round robin for the fairness it already gives you, and check deal counts per consultant periodically through reporting, adjusting who sits in each rotation if someone's consistently carrying more than their share. Each specialism becomes its own rule, stacked above a general catch all. More specialisms simply mean more rules stacked in front of the catch all, each scoped to its own smaller group.

The groups below are generated live from the Level column in the table above. Change a team member's level and the rotations update automatically, so this stays accurate as the team changes.

Auto assignment: destination detection

Not ready to go live yet. Round robin can only route by destination group once something in the data tells it whether an enquiry is Africa/Dubai/Far East/Indian Ocean Islands or Worldwide, and today's data flow doesn't reliably carry that.

The webform's package reference reaches Engage today, but the full package page URL doesn't. That's a known gap on the Engage leg specifically, not a confirmed limitation of the webform or of Zapier as a platform. It may simply be that nobody ever mapped the URL through, since Engage never needed it.

Good news on the URL itself: real package URLs confirm the destination sits in plain English right in the slug, alongside the reference code as its own path segment, for example a Stellenbosch property and a Mozambique property both name the destination directly ahead of the "package" and "nights" wording. That means once the full URL is flowing through, detecting destination is straightforward keyword matching against a short list of place names, not an opaque reference code needing a big lookup table to decode.

The open question worth settling before anything else: does the webform's raw Zapier trigger payload already include the full package URL, even though it's never been mapped anywhere downstream? If Kim can confirm that, adding it to the Freshsales action step already being built into the Zap is a small field mapping addition, not a new leg. If the webform genuinely never captures or sends the full URL at all, that's a bigger piece of work, a hidden field would need adding to the form itself first.

Worth a spot check either way: slugs carry extra noise around the destination (a leading star rating digit, package type wording like "self drive," a nights count, and sometimes a trailing number for duplicate slugs), so a real batch of URLs is worth sampling to confirm place names extract cleanly before relying on it, and to catch city names (Stellenbosch) that need mapping to the right group alongside country names (Mozambique).

Phased approach

  • Phase 1, now: add a Destination Group field to Freshsales (Africa, Dubai, Far East & Indian Ocean Islands / Worldwide), set manually by the consultant at the New or Consulting stage. Round robin routes off that field, no dependency on any Zapier change.
  • Phase 2, near term: extend the Web to Contact data bridge tool with a destination suggestion panel, paste or read in the package URL and match place names against a short destination to group list, so the consultant just confirms a suggestion rather than starting from a cold decision each time.
  • Phase 3, later: once Kim confirms whether the full package URL is present in the raw trigger data, and the place name matching has been proven reliable over real volume, revisit whether Zapier or a Freshsales workflow should auto populate the field.

Worth raising with Kim directly: is this a genuine platform limitation, or a field that was simply never mapped because Engage never asked for it. That single answer decides whether Phase 3 is a quick field mapping addition or a proper build.

10Customer journey: travel readiness touchpoints

A different layer to everything above. This isn't a specific template, it's the content layer underneath the emails, what travel-readiness information actually matters, when it's genuinely useful to share it, and what depends on destination or trip type. This section maps the touchpoints, the copy and exact trigger for each gets built out separately.

Timing matters as much as content. Shared too early, it gets forgotten. Shared too late, there's no runway left to act on it, visas being the clearest example: guidance is only actionable once there's a confirmed itinerary and paid deposit to plan real dates against, so it needs to land as an upfront heads up early, then a firm reminder once those pieces exist.
Destination and trip type decide what's relevant, this can't be one generic checklist sent to every client. A local trip still needs a valid ID and possibly a driver's license if there's a flight or car rental involved, but has no visa or health considerations. A long-haul international trip needs the full set. Getting this right depends on having destination knowledge in place first, which is exactly why this connects back to the auto assignment thinking above, knowing the destination early is what makes it possible to know which of these touchpoints even apply.

The one-year revisit only fires for clients with a positive feedback score on record. Needs a defined threshold (e.g. a minimum star rating or NPS band) and a way to check it at send time, unhappy clients should never get a nostalgic "remember when" email a year after a trip that didn't go well.
Two new Freshsales contact fields needed to make these three work. Date of Birth, populated manually by the agent from the passport copy already collected during booking, no new ask required, it's already in hand, just needs somewhere structured to live. Special Occasion Date plus a Type picklist (Anniversary, Honeymoon, Other), populated by the agent when it comes up naturally in conversation, for example the trip itself being booked as an anniversary or honeymoon trip, never asked for upfront or unprompted. Both are manual entry, not automated capture, so this needs a training point (see Team training) rather than a Zapier or webform change. Worth a quick POPIA check on Date of Birth specifically, since it's sensitive personal data beyond what's collected today and needs a clear purpose tied to it.
The Child Travel Requirements document already exists in branded, print-ready form and covers every scenario (both parents, one parent, non-parent, unaccompanied, alternative care) with a link to the DHA advisory. Worth keeping it, and future documents like it, in the same approved attachment library referenced in the bulk deals section, so agents always grab the current version rather than an old copy floating around inboxes.
